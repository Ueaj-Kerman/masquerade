The model wears its own mask

Fused self-speculative decoding via mask-token self-distillation — no separate drafter, no extra backbone: the base model drafts for itself.

DSpark, DFlash, and EAGLE bolt a separate drafter onto a target model. Masquerade fuses the drafter into the target: run the model over a sequence with a masked region and without, and distill the unmasked (exact next-token) logits into the masked forward — using live weights, so the model is continuously distilling itself, with no reference model. At inference, append [MASK] tokens after the last committed token: one forward of the full model drafts k future tokens, which standard speculative verification then accepts or rejects — provably lossless under greedy decoding.

Method in one figure

train (one forward, doc-packed, multi-region):
  [ctx…][M M M][region…][ctx…][M M][region…]
   • k masks inserted BEFORE each region, carrying the region's rope positions
   • real tokens attend only to real tokens  => the real stream IS the teacher
     (bit-exact to a plain forward — property-tested)
   • masks attend to prior real context + earlier masks of their own region
   • loss: position-decayed TV + hard CE at mask slots vs stop-grad teacher
     logits; optional NTP CE at real slots (the pretraining objective)

decode (static KV cache, CUDA graphs; per-row cache-length visibility):
  draft   [last, M×k]            -> exact p(next) + k draft distributions
  verify  [next, d1..dk]          -> commits accepted+2 tokens per round
  fused   [next, d1..dk, M×k]    -> verify AND draft in ONE forward (B=1)
Full teacher stop-grad matters: detaching hidden states is not enough with tied embeddings — gradients leak through the shared lm_head and let the model make itself easier to predict ("self-simplification"). Fixing this took GSM8K retention from 43.8% to 62.5% at matched acceptance.

Stage 1 — the simple demonstrator (and an honest failure)

Frozen original weights as teacher, one masked region per sample, two forwards per step, on 120k on-policy responses (DSpark’s data recipe). Acceptance climbs steadily; the engine is exactly lossless in fp32 with the trained checkpoint (τ 4.38 verified token-for-token against AR).

Stages 2–3 — live self-distillation, fused & multi-region

The final trainer packs documents, inserts many mask groups per sequence, and gets teacher and student from one forward — the real-token stream is provably identical to a plain causal forward (masks are invisible to it), so live self-distillation comes for free. ~150 supervised draft positions per sequence vs 8 in stage 1.

The fused decode mode (verify + next draft in one forward when the previous round fully accepts) lifts B=1 GSM8K decoding to 533 tok/s vs 269 AR — 1.98× lossless at 2.74 tokens/forward. Control: spec with UNTRAINED masks is slower than AR (233 vs 255) — the speedup comes from training, not machinery.

Stage 4 — DSpark-style Markov head

A rank-256 sequential head (bias = W2·W1[prev token], zero-initialized) conditions each draft position on the previously drafted token via a serial logits-only sweep — same trick as DSpark’s sequential block, at negligible decode cost. An ablation arm (same recipe, head off) isolates its contribution.

variant (same weights)τ gsm8kτ chatτ code

Verdict: the sequential head is redundant on a fused drafter. DSpark’s Markov block compensates for a shallow 5-layer drafter backbone; masquerade’s drafts already flow through all 28 layers of the target, so the low-rank bigram correction adds ≤0.05 τ. This answers the stage-4 question: the fused objective alone already provides the pareto improvement the sequential block was meant to rescue.

Stage 5 — masked pretraining (NTP + mask from scratch)

Same fused objective as a pretraining regularizer: does forcing the model to predict through masked gaps accelerate learning? Qwen3-architecture models trained from scratch on FineWeb with the aurora optimizer (Tilde Research’s leverage-aware Muon variant, their public recipe), NTP-only vs NTP+mask, matched token budgets, three scales (30m GB10 / 50m / 124m H100) plus a w_tv sweep.

Context parallel & systems notes

  • Ring-attention CP (torch experimental): forward parity ~2e-2 rel; 1.43× on 2×H100 at 0.6B/T=16384; comms-dominated below that (0.81× on a tiny model at T=8192). varlen/doc-packed masks + CP remains an upstream boundary (torchtitan documents the same) — fused multi-region runs CP-off, plain-causal paths CP-on.
  • Compiled stage-5 arms sustain ~190k tok/s (50m, T=2048, H100, flex block-sparse attention); the mask objective costs ~4× today (block-mask build + pair loss, unoptimized) — an engineering gap, not a method one.
  • Engine: draft and verify share one [B, k+1] shape (one CUDA graph serves both); mask KV slots are rolled back by per-row visibility and overwritten by verify writes; fused mode drafts and verifies in a single forward when the previous round fully accepted.
Honesty box. (1) Wall-clock numbers use our research engine; vLLM-grade paging would lift the AR baseline and our decode path alike — τ and tokens-per-forward are the engine-independent quantities. (2) The quality/acceptance dial is real: pushing τ above ~5.7 on a 0.6B costs measurable GSM8K today. (3) DSpark trains 10 epochs over 1.3M prompts; our runs are 3000 steps (≈0.4 epochs of 120k) — acceptance was still rising at cutoff. (4) Stage-5 curves compare matched token budgets, not matched wall-clock.
