The model wears its own mask

Fused self-speculative decoding via mask-token self-distillation — no separate drafter, no extra backbone: the base model drafts for itself.

DSpark, DFlash, and EAGLE bolt a separate drafter onto a target model. Masquerade fuses the drafter into the target: run the model over a sequence with a masked region and without, and distill the unmasked (exact next-token) logits into the masked forward — using live weights, so the model is continuously distilling itself, with no reference model. At inference, append [MASK] tokens after the last committed token: one forward of the full model drafts k future tokens, which standard speculative verification then accepts or rejects — provably lossless under greedy decoding.

Method in one figure

decode (static KV cache, CUDA graphs; per-row cache-length visibility):
  draft   [last, M×k]            -> exact p(next) + k draft distributions
  verify  [next, d1..dk]          -> commits accepted+2 tokens per round
  fused   [next, d1..dk, M×k]    -> verify AND draft in ONE forward (B=1)
Full teacher stop-grad matters: detaching hidden states is not enough with tied embeddings — gradients leak through the shared lm_head and let the model make itself easier to predict ("self-simplification"). Fixing it (together with the lr recipe) took 0.6B GSM8K retention from 43.8% to 62.5% at matched acceptance, and enabled the lossless 4B result.

Stage 1 — the simple demonstrator (and an honest failure)

Frozen original weights as teacher, one masked region per sample, two forwards per step, on 120k on-policy responses (DSpark’s data recipe). Acceptance climbs steadily; the engine is exactly lossless in fp32 with the trained checkpoint (τ 4.38 verified token-for-token against AR).

Stages 2–3 — live self-distillation, fused & multi-region

The final trainer packs documents, inserts many mask groups per sequence, and gets teacher and student from one forward — the real-token stream is provably identical to a plain causal forward (masks are invisible to it), so live self-distillation comes for free. ~150 supervised draft positions per sequence vs 8 in stage 1.

The hot [MASK] row (its own parameter at lr 1e-2, muP-style for a brand-new token) speeds up mid-training markedly (agreement 0.64 vs 0.58 at step 1500) and lands at the same endpoint with slightly better quality retention.

Without the anchor and stop-grad, high LR collapses spectacularly: the lr 3e-4 arm reached τ 9.0 (accepts everything) at 0.8% GSM8K — the model made itself trivially predictable. That failure mode is the central hazard of fused self-speculation, and it is controllable.

Stage 4 — DSpark-style Markov head: from “useless” to load-bearing

A rank-256 sequential head (bias = W2·W1[prev token], W2 zero-initialized) conditions each draft position on the previously drafted token via a serial logits-only sweep — DSpark’s sequential-block trick at negligible decode cost. Trained naively (N(0, 0.02) init, body lr), it did nothing. Trained muP-style — W1∼N(0,1) with its own lr 100× the body’s — it became the single largest lift in the recipe:

With the hot head, a division of labor emerges: the mask slots learn context while the head handles local sequential correction — head-off τ (3.84) drops below the cold-head run’s 6.68, where the body had to do everything. Methodological lesson: before concluding a component is redundant, verify it actually trained — parameterization (muP scaling for embedding-like parameters) can invert an ablation.

Stage 5 — masked pretraining (NTP + mask from scratch)

Same fused objective as a pretraining regularizer: does forcing the model to predict through masked gaps accelerate learning? Qwen3-architecture models trained from scratch on FineWeb with the aurora optimizer (Tilde Research’s leverage-aware Muon variant, their public recipe), NTP-only vs NTP+mask, matched token budgets, three scales (30m GB10 / 50m / 124m H100) plus a w_tv sweep.

Verdict: neutral-to-slightly-positive at matched tokens, unresolved at scale. The mask arms sit marginally below NTP through ~90M tokens (e.g. 4.349 vs ~4.36 at 42M) and marginally above by ~170M — differences of 0.01–0.06 nats, well short of decisive. Two caveats cut opposite ways: at matched compute NTP wins today (the mask objective costs ~4× wall-clock unoptimized and ~12% of the token budget), but the hypothesis — earlier long-horizon planning — is one that early FineWeb val loss is nearly blind to, and the fused objective’s side payoff (a model that exits pretraining already able to self-draft) went unmeasured because mask-arm checkpoints were lost mid-run. A clean test needs Chinchilla-scale budgets, frequent checkpoints, and downstream/long-context + acceptance evals.

Context parallel & systems notes

  • Ring-attention CP (torch experimental): forward parity ~2e-2 rel; 1.43× on 2×H100 at 0.6B/T=16384; comms-dominated below that (0.81× on a tiny model at T=8192). varlen/doc-packed masks + CP remains an upstream boundary (torchtitan documents the same) — fused multi-region runs CP-off, plain-causal paths CP-on.
  • Compiled stage-5 arms sustain ~190k tok/s (50m, T=2048, H100, flex block-sparse attention); the mask objective costs ~4× today (block-mask build + pair loss, unoptimized) — an engineering gap, not a method one.
  • Engine: draft and verify share one [B, k+1] shape (one CUDA graph serves both); mask KV slots are rolled back by per-row visibility and overwritten by verify writes; fused mode drafts and verifies in a single forward when the previous round fully accepted.
Honesty box. (1) Wall-clock numbers use our research engine; vLLM-grade paging would lift the AR baseline and our decode path alike — τ and tokens-per-forward are the engine-independent quantities. (2) The quality/acceptance dial is real: pushing τ above ~5.7 on a 0.6B costs measurable GSM8K today. (3) DSpark trains 10 epochs over 1.3M prompts; our runs are 3000 steps (≈0.4 epochs of 120k) — acceptance was still rising at cutoff. (4) Stage-5 curves compare matched token budgets, not matched wall-clock. (5) Three host power failures (PSU trips under sustained 5090 load) truncated the stage-5 mask arms and one 4B arm; all quoted numbers are from runs that completed or from committed checkpoints. (6) The Markov head is 78M params at Qwen3’s 152k vocab (~2% of the 4B target) — far below DSpark’s ~1B drafter but not zero; rank and vocab-factorization are untuned.
